# Prototype Instructions

Run the local server yourself and open the preview in the browser available to this environment. Do not give the user server-start instructions when you can run it.

Before making substantial visual changes, use the Product Design plugin's `get-context` skill when the visual source is unclear or no longer matches the current goal. When the user gives durable prototype-specific design feedback, preferences, or decisions, record them in `AGENTS.md`.

When implementing from a selected generated mock, treat that image as the source of truth for layout, component anatomy, density, spacing, color, typography, visible content, and hierarchy.

## Durable Product Decisions

- The product title is exactly `哎！我改变了历史？`.
- The history picker header uses the project-owned transparent calligraphic wordmark at `/assets/brand/history-wordmark.png`; keep the exact title as its accessible name and do not restore the instructional subtitle `选择你要闯入的瞬间`. Keep only one aligned 44px settings button in the header; filmstrip, two-column table, and audio belong in its accessible secondary menu rather than appearing as separate top-level controls.
- The mobile filmstrip picker follows the selected archival-poster anatomy: synchronized timeline above one centered illustrated scene, a vermilion vertical year rail, a separate warm-paper dossier with the complete place/role/decision/deadline, an independently framed vermilion `闯入这一刻` action, and visible neighboring-card previews. Preserve the generated archival stage, paper, and vermilion textures; do not restore the flat image-plus-overlay card or let the action disappear inside the dossier.
- Filmstrip geometry must be relative to the 320-390px product container, never to the outer browser viewport. A timeline click must atomically center its matching card before the active state is shown; the year readout, active timeline node, and visible centered poster must always name the same seed.
- The player is a modern Chinese traveler, not an abstract observer. The first screen is the complete chronological catalog of one hundred historical moments; there is no profile, personality test, MBTI label, or recommended subset before play.
- Opening cards must be famous, concrete historical turning points, including clearly labelled BCE dates where appropriate, with an exact date, place, assigned role, immediate decision, deadline, and actual outcome.
- The deck must include substantial Chinese history before 1840 and frame world history in language accessible to Chinese players.
- The one-hundred-card deck is fixed at exactly 58 Chinese moments and 42 world moments. Every Chinese moment must occur before 1949; do not include events from 1949 onward in China or political subjects centered on the Chinese Communist Party, the People's Republic of China, or "New China." Soviet history remains allowed as world history. World cards must use people or events broadly recognizable to ordinary Chinese players; do not spend slots on minor-country or specialist Western history.
- Public-memory fiction may inspire a counterfactual hook, but the card itself must label and anchor real history. Preserve dramatic choices without turning sexual violence into a playable entertainment mechanic.
- All 100 history moments belong to one chronological catalog with two synchronized browsing modes: mode A is the horizontal filmstrip and mode B is a vertically scrolling two-column card grid. Both modes share the same current node; mode B provides search plus period, region, and theme filters. Never reintroduce a recommended subset, shuffle, expand-all action, or archive modal.
- The run has one authoritative timeline containing only the player's decisions. Never generate a hidden personality timeline or let a personality system choose on the player's behalf.
- During play the user may use direct rewrites without a per-run quota to declare completed historical results. Each rewrite still consumes the current node's single decision. The declaration is player-authored canon: the client commits the exact text synchronously without a model approval or classification request; DeepSeek only performs the creative world simulation in the following node.
- Preserve the current coal, newsprint, vermilion, teal, and yellow visual system unless the user explicitly requests a new visual direction.
- A complete run has exactly 12 playable decisions within one protagonist's biological lifetime. The alternate 2026 report is a separate posthumous epilogue and never consumes one of the twelve nodes.
- The protagonist keeps one fixed name and body across all twelve nodes, ages monotonically, may change role, faction, city, and social reach, and dies after the twelfth decision. Never use consciousness relay, reincarnation, descendants, or a replacement identity as the playable character.
- Early nodes provide tight feedback on the scale of the same day, three days, and six weeks. Later nodes spread across the same person's career and late life; modern starts compress the lifespan so the death still occurs before the 2026 report.
- After node 3, the opening event cannot remain the current plot, title, objective, or role. It may be named once as a causal source when the current title, task, role, institution, and conflict have genuinely changed. The same protagonist must enter a different consequential conflict caused by earlier choices rather than randomly changing topic.
- Surprise comes from changing the social carrier, conflict, institution, or affected population, not from forced travel. A timeline may remain in China; prefer real people, institutions, cities, and lived experiences familiar to Chinese players, while changing at least two narrative dimensions from the recent three nodes.
- Active gameplay always provides an exit control that clears the run and returns to the one-hundred-moment browser while preserving its mode, filters, and current node for the session.
- Event screens target the iPhone 13 viewport at 390 x 844 and show only the scene, complete event copy, causal proof, all three generated choices, and the compact fourth-path entry. Prompt-target turns of at most 180 characters should fit without scrolling; only accepted 181-230-character overflow may scroll inside the content layer so nothing clips. Do not restore the four-metric dashboard or repeated mission explanations.
- Scene art must follow the simulated year as well as the chapter. Never show modern or space technology in a pre-industrial node merely because it is a late chapter.
- A refresh or development-server reconnect during generation must resume the saved AI request automatically. Harmless non-narrative drift may be normalized locally, but visible historical prose is always AI-authored. A parseable response repairs only its invalid top-level AI fields and merges them back into the untouched scene; field-level validation must report missing fields and invalid narrative fields together so one compact patch can fix both. If that patch still fails, allow one evidence-guided full recovery; after three invalid AI outputs, preserve the run and show an explicit retry instead of inventing a generic local historical scene.
- DeepSeek turn and ending requests use an 8192-token output ceiling so transport truncation cannot strand normal JSON; concise output is controlled by the prompt and schema, not a small token cap.
- Loading-room motion includes archival drift, causal-thread tracing, pulse nodes, exposure passes, and status cycling, with a `prefers-reduced-motion` static fallback.
- Loading progress is monotonic and honest: connected, reasoning, writing, validating, and repairing states come from the actual streamed request rather than timers. The steps and track never loop back or pretend to reach 100%. Use `新历史正在成形` rather than `因果推演` as the player-facing frame.
- Each of the 100 cards owns a fixed, schema-validated first turn and enters it immediately without a model request; it must show `固定历史开场`. Turns 2-12 are model-generated and must show `DeepSeek 实时生成`. Local deterministic code may preserve a player-authored rewrite receipt, but it must never invent a continuation scene or a 2026 report.
- The filmstrip and grid are two views of the same chronological catalog, never separate archives. Selecting in either view updates the same current seed; filtering may hide the current card but must not silently replace it.
- Every history seed owns a local `/assets/history/<seed-id>.webp` path with an event-accurate source or a local period-art fallback; broken remote images must never be part of runtime rendering.
- The picker timeline and card filmstrip are one synchronized control: scrolling cards centers the matching year, and selecting a year scrolls the matching card into view.
- In filmstrip mode, the timeline uses one long centered continuous axis with every node center fixed exactly on the line and the active year label in a dedicated row below it. The active history card is one continuous poster: the scene and ragged dossier share the same width, the dossier overlaps the scene instead of becoming a smaller second rectangle, and the action remains a related but separate vermilion slab.
- History picker cards must leave a clearly visible preview of the next card on the right at first glance. Keep the parenthetical swipe hint `（滑动可切换不同的历史瞬间）` visible beside the year/count readout.
- Active play is a continuous cinematic surface, not a dashboard. Use one scene, one compact causal receipt, and three separated action rows; avoid nested cards, metrics, badges, and explanatory panels.
- The AI is framed as a grounded world simulator. It generates the three ordinary actions against historical constraints, but it never vetoes a direct player rewrite. For direct rewrites, the client owns the immediate receipt and the AI owns only the next-node world extrapolation. The visible proof is source provenance, a causal receipt, and the same protagonist's age and evolving role, not long model prose.
- Personality is not part of the product. Do not restore profile forms, MBTI dimensions, personality-tailored choices, causal-preview personality lenses, or personality comparison reports.
- Music begins on the first eligible user gesture in the filmstrip and continues through generation, events, and ending. Browser autoplay failure must never block play.
- A direct rewrite enters the timeline synchronously as an exact client-owned fact, so it has no approval request that can fail or consume time. The next AI scene must visibly apply that fact, while network failure, model repair, refresh recovery, and retry can never erase or weaken it; player-authored results enter the same causal memory and 2026 ending contract as generated choices.
- Every one of the 12 playable nodes must be a consequential alternate-history turning point. Do not use ordinary reporting, routine administration, or generic livelihood scenes merely to create variety.
- The twelfth node is the protagonist's final playable decision, not a death cutscene. After that decision becomes canon, the ending must show the protagonist's death, four posthumous historical eras, three concrete 2026 life details, and a novel-like closing passage about a world the protagonist never saw.
- Every completed action enters a deterministic client-owned world canon before the next model call. Player-authored rewrites are irreversible facts, retain their exact wording, and remain explicit active mandates for the next three nodes; multiple mandates such as accession plus industrialization must coexist rather than collapse into one label.
- The client must never choose the next conflict from a fixed list of plot categories. DeepSeek receives the complete immutable canon, recent scenes, protagonist age, and historical constraints, then performs open-ended first-, second-, and third-order inference to choose the next consequential conflict.
- DeepSeek is the scene director, while the client owns continuity. Every turn must include at least two era-accurate historical anchors and three executable actions with actor, action, target, and deadline. Generic actions are rejected for model repair; failure remains retryable and never becomes a generic local scene.
- Normal continuation calls use DeepSeek V4 Flash non-thinking mode with the same full prompt, schema, 8192-token ceiling, and 90-second transport window. A field patch also uses the fast path; only a failed patch escalates once to high reasoning. The ending keeps high reasoning and uses two concurrent requests with disjoint ownership: biography and posthumous 2026 world report. Never split one scene into blind parallel copy fragments.
- Healthy requests are never delayed. HTTP 429, 5xx, and network failures receive at most two short exponential retries with jitter and bounded `Retry-After` support; cancellation stays immediate. A provider outage must preserve the run and remain explicitly retryable rather than fabricate history.
- DeepSeek continuation calls for nodes 2-12 share one stable static system-protocol prefix so context caching can reuse invariant rules; all changing canon and chapter data stays in the final user message. Turn responses stream over SSE, but token fragments never enter persisted game state.
- Continuation context is tiered rather than raw chat history: send every prior decision as a compact life index, the latest decision in full, the latest three consequences in detail, every player-authored rewrite verbatim, the latest persistent ledger, and the latest three scenes only for anti-repetition. Never resend all prior scene prose.
- The biography ending receives all twelve years, ages, roles, decisions, direct results and costs. The 2026 report receives the complete decision canon and final persistent world ledger. The client merges both independently validated reports into one ending.
- Event screens place the three actions before a compact bottom `历史对照`. It must stack `你的时间线`, `正史原本`, and `为何改变` as three full-width rows. Each conclusion is scan copy, not an essay: alternate state and causal bridge are at most 36 Chinese characters, the factual baseline is at most 48, and the prior choice is never repeated here. The real-history field never repeats the UI label or mixes in the alternate timeline; never restore the square `因果回执` block or repeated `你的决定 / 已经改变 / 重大节点` labels.
- Prompt every newly generated event `narrative` toward 80-180 Chinese characters in two to five complete sentences, preferably three. The client accepts complete narratives up to 230 characters without mutation; anything longer enters field repair instead of being sliced. Across those sentences it must establish the famous-event prehistory or prior causal bridge, name the real people, institution or faction and visible evidence, then state the protagonist's usable leverage, immediate decision, and direct cost of failure. Never line-clamp, ellipsize, or silently hide AI-authored event prose. Loading an older save may preserve its shorter original narrative verbatim.
- The validated-scene generating page may scroll vertically to preserve the complete AI-authored narrative. Its `下一步` confirmation remains in normal document flow after the full narrative and scene metadata, never floating over or hiding the copy.
- Once a generated scene is validated and waiting for `下一步`, remove the generating page's former 72px top spacer and use a 34px gap between a player-authored canon receipt and the new scene (half the former 68px). At 390 × 844 the complete normal-length scene and confirmation button should be directly visible without an initial scroll; loading-only layouts keep their existing breathing room.
- Event pages use an adaptive vertical height system at 390 x 844: the progress header occupies normal flow above the scene image, while the translucent coal content surface rises over roughly 35-40% of the image to fund readable type without hiding the artwork. Event prose stays at 13-14px, actions at 14-15px, and supporting evidence at 11-13px. Prompt-target 180-character turns should fit without scrolling; a legal overflow up to the 230-character client ceiling must remain completely readable through vertical scrolling rather than clip. The fourth-path button keeps an 8px bottom safe area.
- Mobile readability takes priority over squeezing reports into microtype. Picker, loading, event, result-confirmation, rewrite sheet, biography, and 2026 report copy must use an 11px visible minimum with larger body/action type. Final reports keep all generated text and may scroll inside their report reading area; never restore 7-9px prose, line clamps, or ellipses to force a report into one viewport.
- `2026，普通人的一天` is one natural paragraph assembled from exactly three complete AI-authored life details of 12-18 characters each. The client never slices report prose by character count; details outside that range or ending as sentence fragments go through field repair.
- Scene captions always use two full-width rows: the complete year/life-stage/age string on the first row and the location on the second. Never return to a left-right caption that lets a long location squeeze or split the time.
- The ending has exactly two report pages for the same player timeline. Page one is a `史记`-style biography spanning all twelve decisions with both modern Mandarin and classical Chinese. Page two is a one-screen novel-like butterfly-effect account from the protagonist's death to ordinary life in 2026. Neither page may mention personality.
- The public repository must install and build from an arbitrary user directory on Windows and Linux. Runtime files, package configuration, and scripts must never contain a developer-specific absolute home path; keep `npm run check:portability` and the cross-platform GitHub Actions build green.
- Historical locations must use vocabulary appropriate to their year and culture. For pre-1900 scenes, reject modern generic space names such as `议事厅`, `会议室`, `办公室`, and `指挥中心`; repair the AI-authored `location` field instead of locally rewriting visible prose.
- The immediate post-decision screen for generated A/B/C actions is a plain result confirmation, not a system-analysis report. A player-authored rewrite skips this screen and moves directly into generation of the next node; never show fixed `代价`, `受益者`, `承担者`, or propagation analysis for a rewrite. Never show `因果回响`, `世界已回应`, per-step deviation points, or low-contrast microcopy.
- Loading screens must stay inside the fiction. Do not name DeepSeek in loading notes or narrate internal mechanics such as propagation analysis, beneficiaries, costs, JSON validation, or model work. Model provenance remains visible only on the completed event itself.
- Saving either final report renders the current report's complete scroll width and height at 2x resolution. Desktop browsers download the PNG directly. Mobile Web first prepares the image, then a second user action opens the system share sheet with clear instructions to choose the platform's save-to-photos action and an explicit PNG download fallback; never claim that a browser can universally request photo-library write permission or guarantee direct album insertion.
- A stability release must pass the explicit real `npm run test:soak` suite with ten different starting moments, twelve decisions per run, four to five globally unique direct rewrites per run, at least nine complete dual-report endings, and zero local playable-scene or ending fallbacks. Keep soak artifacts and raw model payloads out of Git.
- A validated continuation scene must never auto-reveal. The loading room keeps the complete validated scene visible and exposes `下一步`; only that explicit player action enters the event screen. A refresh while waiting preserves the ready scene and still requires the same confirmation.
- The 2026 report must preserve complete AI-authored sentences. Never locally slice, line-clamp, or ellipsize report prose; prompts keep the target concise while schemas allow modest complete overruns and reject sentence fragments for field repair. A legacy save containing a truncated report preserves all twelve decisions and regenerates only the ending.
- Saving either final report must render the current report's complete scroll width and height at high resolution; never export only the visible mobile viewport or reintroduce clipping to make the image shorter.
- Compact history-comparison fields may select one independent complete clause already written by the model or remove dispensable aspect words, but must never cut by character count, promote a conditional prefix such as `如果...` into a fact, alter player-authored canon, or rewrite historical substance locally.
- A/B/C keeps the model's full `label` as the exact canonical decision and derives a separate `displayLabel` of at most 36 Chinese characters for the fixed-height mobile row. If the full label is longer, the first tap must show the complete canonical text and require an explicit confirmation before committing it; a compact label may never replace or weaken the stored decision.
